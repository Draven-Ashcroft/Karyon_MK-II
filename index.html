Eukaryotic Cell

A eukaryotic cell contains many membrane-bound organelles. The nucleus houses the genome and is the site of transcription — the process we are about to zoom into.

›
🧬

Nucleus

Bounded by a double membrane (the nuclear envelope) perforated by nuclear pore complexes. Inside, the nucleoplasm houses chromatin and is where RNA Polymerase II transcribes mRNA.

›
🧬

DNA — Double Helix

DNA in the nucleoplasm coils through the space around the nucleolus. The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary base pairs.

›
⚙️

RNA Polymerase II

Tracks along the DNA template, unwinding the helix just ahead of the active site. The strand glows where transcription is currently occurring.

›
🧵

hnRNA — Exons & Introns

The growing transcript is hnRNA: green segments are exons (retained), red segments are introns (spliced out before the mature mRNA is exported).

›
🎩

5′ Capping

Addition of methyl-7-guanosine (m⁷G) phosphate to the 5′ end of the mRNA.

🧵

Poly-A Tailing

Addition of many adenylate residues at the tail region (the 3′ poly-A tail).

✂️

Splicing

Removal of introns (non-coding sequences) and joining of exons by means of spliceosomes.

🔬

Translation

The ribosome assembles on the mRNA to begin protein synthesis.
